# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## DO's

1. **Do** keep your profile professional – use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests.
2. **Do** share academic achievements, projects, and certifications – these build your credibility and attract recruiters.
3. **Do** think before you post – ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?"
4. **Do** use privacy settings – restrict personal posts to friends only; keep professional profiles (LinkedIn, GitHub) public.
5. **Do** engage positively – comment respectfully, support peers, and participate in meaningful discussions.
6. **Do** report cyberbullying or harassment immediately using the platform's built-in report tools.
7. **Do** credit others' work – if you share someone's idea, research, or image, always give proper attribution.

## DON'Ts

1. **Don't** share your personal information publicly – avoid posting your phone number, home address, or Aadhaar/ID details.
2. **Don't** post offensive, discriminatory, or hateful content – it can have serious academic and legal consequences.
3. **Don't** spread unverified news or rumours – always check the source before sharing any information.
4. **Don't** accept friend/follow requests from strangers without verifying their identity.
5. **Don't** use social media to vent frustration about classmates, faculty, or your institution – this can seriously damage relationships and your reputation.
6. **Don't** post during exams or important deadlines – protect your time and focus.
7. **Don't** plagiarize – copying others' posts or research without credit is both unethical and potentially illegal.
