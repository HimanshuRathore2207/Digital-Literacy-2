# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account – use a mix of uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across platforms.
2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts (email, social media, banking apps) – this adds an extra layer of security even if your password is stolen.
3. **Do not share your OTP with anyone** – no bank, UPI app, or government agency will ever call you and ask for your OTP.
4. **Keep your privacy settings updated** – restrict who can see your posts, location, and personal information on social media platforms.
5. **Think before you click** – do not click on unknown links sent via WhatsApp, SMS, or email, even if they appear to come from friends.
6. **Block and report cyberbullying** – if someone is harassing you online, use the platform's block and report tools immediately. Do not engage with the bully.

## Financial / UPI Safety

7. **Never scan a QR code to receive money** – QR codes are for paying, not for receiving. Scanning an unknown QR code can debit money from your account.
8. **Verify before transferring** – always double-check the UPI ID, phone number, and recipient name before confirming any payment, even in urgent-seeming situations.

## Additional Tips

9. **Log out from shared devices** – always log out of your accounts when using college computer labs or borrowed devices.
10. **Report cybercrime immediately** – do not wait or feel embarrassed. Report at the National Cyber Crime Portal: **cybercrime.gov.in** or call the 24x7 helpline **1930**.
